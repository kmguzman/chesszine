ABOUT

Check, Check, Checkmate

The reason I wanted to make a Zine is simple, I love chess. I want to share that joy everyone around me. Another reason is, I believe chess gets a bad reputation. When people think of chess in New York City, they think of the homeless chess hustler trying to cheat them in a game of chess for money. But there is much more to chess in NYC than that. Hopefully this short zine highlighted some key aspects of chess in NYC. I hope you see you in one of these free checks parks, where no one is allowed to gamble on chess, and everyone plays fair.

Chess is a game of give and take. To best summarize that notion I'll leave you with a quote from the greatest American chess player to had ever lived, Paul Morphy. "The ability to play chess is the sign of a gentleman. The ability to play chess well is the sign of a wasted life"...talk about duality

DID YOU KNOW?

The word “Checkmate” in Chess comes from the Persian phrase “Shah Mat,” which means “the King is dead.”.
